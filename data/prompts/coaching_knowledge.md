# Coaching Knowledge Base

## Drill Prescriptions for Common Mechanical Issues

### Arm Path / Arm Slot
- **Towel drill**: Hold a rolled towel in throwing hand, go through full delivery aiming to snap the towel at a target. Builds arm path consistency without ball stress. Focus on elbow leading the hand and a smooth, repeatable release point.
- **Wall drill (arm spiral)**: Stand sideways to a wall at arm's length. Practice the arm action from hand break to high-cock position, letting the hand brush the wall. Reinforces proper arm spiral and prevents short-arming.

### Posture / Trunk Tilt
- **Balance drill (stork holds)**: Lift lead leg to balance position and hold for 3-5 seconds. Pitcher should be tall through the torso with head over belly button. Builds postural stability and proprioception at the top of the delivery.
- **Flat ground with pause**: Throw from flat ground, pausing at balance point and again at foot plant before finishing the throw. Emphasizes staying tall and controlled through each phase.

### Hip-Shoulder Separation
- **Hip-lead drill (step-behinds)**: From the stretch, step behind with the lead foot (crossing behind the rubber), then stride toward the plate. Forces the hips to open ahead of the shoulders, building separation feel.
- **Rocker drill**: Rock forward and back on the mound, emphasizing leading with the hip into stride while keeping the shoulders closed. Throw at 50-70% effort. Develops the sequencing of hips firing before the trunk.

### Lead Leg Block
- **Brace drill (wall push)**: Place lead foot against a wall or immovable object, simulate delivery and feel the firm front side. The lead leg should brace and resist, not collapse.
- **Downhill throws**: Throw from a slight downhill slope to naturally encourage firmer lead leg landing and better energy transfer.

### Timing / Kinetic Chain
- **Crow hop throws**: Long-toss with crow hops to reinforce proximal-to-distal sequencing (legs drive first, arm follows). Builds the feel of the kinetic chain firing in order.
- **Connection ball drill**: Place a small ball or glove between the lead arm and torso. If it drops early, the upper body is flying open. Teaches staying connected and sequenced.

## Red Flag Patterns

When these patterns appear in the metrics, flag them and map to coaching language:

| Metric Pattern | Red Flag Name | What to Check | Coaching Cue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Low hip-shoulder separation at foot plant (<20 deg) | "Hip is leaking" | Back hip opening too early; front side pulling open | "Lead with your hip, keep your chest closed longer" |
| Back knee collapsing during stride | "Back side collapse" | Back knee angle dropping significantly; loss of drive | "Stay strong on your back leg, push the ground away" |
| Low arm slot / low shoulder abduction (<70 deg) | "Arm is dragging low" | Elbow below shoulder at foot plant; late arm action | "Get your elbow up to shoulder height as your foot lands" |
| Excessive trunk forward tilt at release (>45 deg) | "Falling off the mound" | Over-rotation or weak lead leg block | "Finish tall, throw through the catcher not into the ground" |
| Very high elbow varus moment | "Elbow stress pattern" | Excessive force on the UCL; may be timing or arm path | "This is an injury risk area — prioritize arm care and rest" |
| Lead knee collapsing at release | "Soft front side" | Lead leg not bracing; energy leaking | "Land firm, push into the ground with your front leg" |
| Late trunk rotation (low rotational velocity) | "Arm is doing all the work" | Trunk not contributing; over-reliance on arm speed | "Let your body do the throwing — rotate your trunk, don't just throw with your arm" |

## Age-Appropriate Coaching Progressions

### Pre-Pubescent (ages 8-11)
- **Focus**: Movement quality ONLY
- **Priorities**: Balance, rhythm, arm path, having fun
- **Avoid**: Velocity targets, force comparisons, mechanical overload
- **Cues**: Simple, one-at-a-time ("balance and throw", "elbow up", "finish your pitch")
- **Drills**: Balance holds, flat ground catch, towel drill, target throwing games

### Early Adolescent (ages 12-13)
- **Focus**: Movement quality + consistency
- **Add**: Pitch-to-pitch repeatability, basic kinetic chain sequencing
- **Avoid**: Max-effort training, weighted balls, velocity chasing
- **Cues**: Can layer two concepts ("lead with your hip AND stay tall")
- **Drills**: All of above + rocker drill, connection ball, basic long toss

### Mid-Adolescent (ages 14-15)
- **Focus**: Movement quality + strength integration
- **Add**: Kinetic chain efficiency, lead leg block, output metrics (with context)
- **Begin**: Structured strength training (bodyweight first), mound work refinement
- **Cues**: More technical language is OK ("hip-shoulder separation", "arm slot")
- **Drills**: All of above + brace drills, crow hops, weighted ball introduction (supervised)

### Late Adolescent (ages 16-17)
- **Focus**: Approaching adult-level comparisons
- **Add**: Full kinetic analysis, velocity development, pitch design basics
- **OK to discuss**: Arm stress metrics, force production, efficiency ratios
- **Cues**: Full biomechanical terminology appropriate
- **Drills**: Full training program, plyocare balls, pull-downs, bullpen work

## Coach-Friendly Language Mappings

Translate technical metrics into language coaches and parents understand:

| Technical Term | Coach-Friendly Version |
|---|---|
| Hip-shoulder separation | "Loading" or "hip lead" |
| Shoulder external rotation | "Arm layback" or "arm cocking" |
| Lead knee extension velocity | "Front leg firmness" or "leg block" |
| Trunk anterior tilt | "Leaning forward" or "posture" |
| Trunk lateral tilt | "Tilting sideways" or "side bend" |
| Torso rotational velocity | "Trunk speed" or "body rotation" |
| Elbow varus moment | "Elbow stress" or "inside elbow force" |
| Shoulder internal rotation moment | "Shoulder load" or "rotator cuff stress" |
| Shoulder horizontal abduction | "Arm position behind the body" |
| Stride length as % of height | "How far you step toward the plate" |
| Kinetic chain sequencing | "Timing" or "everything firing in order" |
| Proximal-to-distal sequencing | "Big muscles first, arm last" |
